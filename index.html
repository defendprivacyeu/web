FAQs

What are AML Laws?
AML laws, also known as anti-money laundering laws, exist to prevent the financing of crime around the world. AML laws limit the use of cash in transactions, the use of prepaid debit cards, the use of anonymizing services in cryptocurrencies, and govern know-your-customer verifications for financial businesses. Under the EU's current AML regime, it is growing ever more complicated to transact privately in the global financial system for EU citizens.

How do AML laws affect our human rights?
EU AML laws mandate the identification of every EU citizen in the global financial system. Our financial transactions provide a real-time biography of our lives, laying bare our political affiliations, our health condition, our religious and sexual orientation, the places we frequently visit and our location, as well as the people we frequently interact with. The extent of such real-time surveillance has proven impacts on the enactment of our human rights, from our freedom of expression to our freedom of movement and our freedom of choice.

How much crime do AML laws prevent and how much do they cost the taxpayer?

How does taking the European Parliament to court work?
European institutions are accountable to the European Court of Justice (ECJ). The ECJ exists to ensure that EU laws are consistently interpreted and applied across member countries. In 2023, the ECJ handled 821 new cases for the infringement on proceedings, failures to act, annulments, appeals, and more.

What are the goals of #DefendPrivacyEU?
We are taking the European Parliament to court to demand the annulment of unjust AML laws. We believe that EU AML laws are unproportionate and demand that the EU follows due process in evaluating the efficiency of AML policy and its effects on human rights, GDPR, and the presumption of innocence.
Learn more
What are the main arguments #DefendPrivacyEU is raising?
According to Article 5 of the Maastricht Treaty, any actions applied by the EU shall not exceed what is necessary to achieve the objective of the Treaties. We believe that the European Parliament has violated Article 5 by passing AML laws which infringe on our lawfully guaranteed liberties to enforce inefficient legislation. The EU has not followed due process, lacking impact studies, studies on human rights, and assessments of the efficiency of previous regulations.
